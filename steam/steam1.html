This orientation-level course will begin with basic STEAM concepts. STEAM is an acronym for Science, Technology, Engineering, Art, and Mathematics. By blending the five subject areas together, we can begin to see how education is used in everyday life. Utilizing STEAM will begin by learning the engineering design process. Students will be in control of their own education and work process. Students will set small goals every day that will allow them to meet the overall larger goal by the end of the project.

S.T.E.A.M. 1 Projects:

  • Roman Aqueducts
  • Marshmallow Launcher
  • Scratch Programming


Roman Aqueducts


Students will be tasked with completing a roman aqueduct system. They will each create their own individual aqueduct based on the ones used in ancient Rome. Students will then connect their aqueducts to ensure that the aqueducts will reach the empty bucket at the end of the path. Students will work together to build a landscape around the aqueduct that aligns with architecture from Ancient Rome.

Marshmallow Launcher


Students will be responsible for designing, building and testing a catapult. The catapult will launch a marshmallow at a target that is 5 meters away. The student who has the highest total points after launching 5 marshmallows will earn 5 additional points on the assignment. Students will write a report based around their project.

Scratch Programming


Students will create a program that celebrates any holiday of their choosing. When developing the scratch project, students should consider the following: 2 sprites and one cannot be a cat, three scripts total (not necessarily 3 per sprite), one conditional statement, one loop statement, one variable, and one custom block that takes at least one input.
