This second level course will begin with basic STEAM concepts. STEAM is an acronym for Science, Technology, Engineering, Art, and Mathematics. By blending the five subject areas together, we can begin to see how education is used in everyday life. Utilizing STEAM will begin by learning the engineering design process. Students will be in control of their own education and work process. Students will set small goals every day that will allow them to meet the overall larger goal by the end of the project.

S.T.E.A.M. 2 Projects:

  • Paper Rollercoaster
  • Mousetrap Car
  • Popsicle Bridge


Paper Rollercoaster


Students are tasked with creating the longest roller coaster that they possibly can before the marble comes to a complete stop. Materials will need to be purchased from the shop and funds for purchasing those materials will be limited. Roller coasters will be voted on for various categories and whoever has the highest composite score will receive 5 extra points on the assignment. Students will also be responsible for writing a report based on their research and findings.

Mouse Trap Car


Students will utilize the engineering design method to create a car that is powered by a single mouse trap. Students will be required to write a report based on the results of their car. The farthest car will receive extra points by the end. Students throughout the school will have the chance to vote on the best decorated car.

Popsicle Bridge


You will build a bridge out of popsicles that will span across a 24 in gap between two tables. You will be given 250 popsicle sticks in order to complete this challenge. The bridge will need to hold 2000 grams for 1 minute, if you wish to test your bridge to see how much weight it will hold after that we can find more weights.
Bridges should be both architecturally sound as well engineered well. Be creative with how your bridge looks. You will need to paint the popsicles so that they the bridge is aesthetically pleasing as well.
