S.T.E.A.M. Classes

This orientation-level course will begin with basic STEAM concepts. STEAM is an acronym for Science, Technology, Engineering, Art, and Mathematics. By blending the five subject areas together, we can begin to see how education is used in everyday life. Utilizing STEAM will begin by learning the engineering design process. Students will be in control of their own education and work process. Students will set small goals every day that will allow them to meet the overall larger goal by the end of the project.

S.T.E.A.M. 1 Projects:
  • Roman Aqueducts
  • Marshmallow Launcher
  • Video Tour

Science, Technology, Engineering, Art, and Mathematics (S.T.E.A.M.) level 5 is a class that allows students to choose their own path from the start. There are no assigned projects for this class and students can choose to work with 3d printers, robotics, website development, programming, and more. This class is meant to allow students to be creatvive with their high school career. Students who take this class will have successfully completed the first two years of S.T.E.A.M. and will have a firm grasp on the engineering process.

The culmination of the S.T.E.A.M. program begins with level 7 where students will choose one of two projects. The first option is to complete the Rube Goldberg challenge sponsored by the Network of Academic and Scholastic Esports Federations (NASEF) and the second option is to complete a physical Rube Goldberg Machine for The Rube Goldberg Institute for Innovation and Creativity. Students who take this class will also need to take S.T.E.A.M. 8 in order to complete these projects.

S.T.E.A.M. 7 Projects:
  • Minecraft Rube Goldberg Machine
  • Rube Goldberg Machine

The last level of Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (S.T.E.A.M.) allows students to continue working on the project they started in S.T.E.A.M. level 7. Students will complete and submit their Rube Goldberg Machines to their respective contests by the end of the semester. For more information see the websites provided in the links below.
